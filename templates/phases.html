Phases of the Menstrual Cycle

If you're new to understanding menstruation, you're not alone! Menstruation — or a "period" — is a natural biological process that prepares the body for pregnancy. Each month, the uterus builds a lining to host a fertilized egg. If there's no pregnancy, this lining sheds, resulting in bleeding. The menstrual cycle typically spans 28 days and includes four key phases:

1. Menstrual Phase

What happens? The uterine lining sheds. This is when you see bleeding. It usually lasts 3–7 days.

Hormones: Estrogen and progesterone are at their lowest.

Feelings: Tired, bloated, sometimes moody or achy.

Tips: Use a heating pad, hydrate, rest. Be gentle — emotionally and physically.

2. Follicular Phase

What happens? Hormones rise, the uterus rebuilds its lining, and energy levels improve. This phase starts on the first day of your period and lasts about two weeks.

Hormones: Estrogen starts climbing.

Feelings: More focused, fresh, and productive.

Tips: Great time for learning new things or creative work.

3. Ovulation Phase

What happens? The ovary releases an egg. This is the most fertile time.

Hormones: Estrogen peaks, and LH surges to release the egg.

Feelings: Confident, energized, flirty!

Tips: Perfect for social plans or date nights.

4. Luteal Phase

What happens? The body preps for pregnancy. If it doesn’t happen, hormones drop, triggering PMS.

Hormones: Progesterone rises, then falls.

Feelings: Emotional, tired, bloated.

Tips: Be kind, avoid big decisions, eat chocolate 🍫

🤔 Common Questions

  • Is it okay to swim on your period? Yes! Just use a tampon or menstrual cup.
  • Is period blood dirty? Not at all — it's natural and clean.
  • Can stress affect periods? Yes, it can delay or alter the cycle.

🛑 Mythbusters

  • MYTH: You can’t get pregnant during your period.
    FACT: It’s rare, but possible.
  • MYTH: All periods are 28 days.
    FACT: Every body is different — cycles range from 21–35 days.
  • MYTH: Exercising on your period is harmful.
    FACT: Light exercise actually helps cramps and mood.
